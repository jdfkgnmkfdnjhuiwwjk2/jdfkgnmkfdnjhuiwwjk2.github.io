Map Information

How the YegSirens map works

This guide explains what the map shows, how incidents are placed, what call notes mean and how privacy is handled.

Departments and areas shown

This map shows calls from the following fire departments and areas:

  • Edmonton, Alberta
  • St. Albert, Alberta
  • Parkland County, Alberta

Incidents are logged from publicly accessible data. Any times mentioned in each call may not be accurate due to delays in transmission.

Privacy and sensitive calls

Medical calls and some other sensitive call types are moved to the closest intersection to help protect privacy.

Although a marker may appear closer to a house or property, medical calls are intentionally placed at the intersection of nearby streets and not at the exact incident location.

For takedown or move requests for a certain call, please contact yegsirens@gmail.com immediately.

Purpose of the information

This information shall not be used against another individual. All information is provided for the simple education and awareness of citizens in the city and county areas YegSirens accounts for.

YegSirens tries to provide details on every call whenever possible. Sometimes information cannot be received, and some calls on the map may be left with limited or no details.

Understanding apparatus notes

When looking at call notes, you may see one of the following terms followed by a number:

PUMPLADDERENGINETANKERRESCUECAR

These are trucks or units dispatched to the call. The number after the term usually indicates the station assignment of that truck.

For example, PUMP 1 is a pump truck operating out of Fire Station 1 in Edmonton on 10351 96 Street.

Symbols in call notes

~ Dispatcher information

When call notes show a ~ before information, that means the note was specifically provided by the dispatcher at that time.

* YegSirens note

When call notes show a * before information, that means the note was added by YegSirens, often when something is uncertain or needs extra context.

How calls are placed and labelled

Icons are matched as closely as possible to the call type. Medical calls use a medical bag icon, fire calls use a fire symbol, fire alarm calls use a triangle symbol with a purple background, and assistance calls use a green background with a triangle symbol.

The names of each call are titled exactly as provided by Fire Dispatch whenever possible. If YegSirens is unaware of the call type, it may be labelled Incident Unknown.

In some cases, fire crews are dispatched to incidents labelled Problem Unknown. When this happens, YegSirens tries to provide as much detail as possible in the description based on information available at the time.
